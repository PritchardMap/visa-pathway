## Design Context

### Users
Immigrants and permanent residents in South Africa who have held their PR for 5+ years and are preparing to apply for citizenship by naturalisation. They're navigating a confusing, documentation-heavy government process with scattered official information. They're often anxious, time-pressed, and may have English as a second language. They use this during daytime working hours, often on mobile while running errands (visiting Home Affairs, embassies). They come to this site with relief — finally, a clear guide.

### Brand Personality
Trustworthy, navigable, human — "the knowledgeable friend who's already been through this process and laid it all out clearly."

Anti-references: generic government website blandness, cold corporate consulting look, dark techy startup aesthetic.

References: a well-designed civic guide, like a warm migration law office brochure — authoritative but welcoming.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme**: Light — warm off-white, SA sunlight tones. Users are dealing with stressful bureaucracy; light feels official, clear, and reassuring.
- **Color palette**: Warm amber/ochre primary accent (SA earth/gold), deep forest green secondary (SA landscape), warm off-white surfaces — all in OKLCH.
- **Typography**: Literata (warm, humanistic serif) for headings + Figtree (clean, friendly geometric sans) for body — both embody the warmth + clarity pairing.
- **Layout**: Content-heavy but clear hierarchy. Cards used sparingly. Wide readable columns. Step indicators prominent. Progress visible at all times.
- **Motion**: Subtle, purposeful. Accordion opens smoothly. Step transitions ease in/out. No bounce or elastic.

### Design Principles
1. **Clarity above cleverness** — Every word and element should reduce anxiety, not add to it. Plain language. No jargon.
2. **Specificity earns trust** — Exact costs, addresses, phone numbers, and processing times. Vague guidance is useless here.
3. **Warm authority** — Official enough to be trusted, human enough to feel approachable. Think: the best Home Affairs officer you've ever met.
4. **Progress is a gift** — Users should always know where they are, how far they've come, and what's next. Progress indicators on everything.
5. **Mobile first** — Users will check this on their phones while standing in queues at police stations and embassies.
